Timeline

  • 1927  — Cesar Estrada Chavez born near Yuma, Arizona
  • 1942  — Chavez begins as a farm worker
  • 1944  Chavez joins the National Farm Workers Union, his first
  • 1946  Chavez and his family join the National Farm Workers Labor Union.
  • 1948  Chavez becomes a community organizer for Communtiy Service Organization(CSO), a Latino civil rights group.
  • 1952  Chavez organizes strikes, marches, and a boycott of merchants in Oxnard to protest local unemployment.
  • 1958  Chavez leaves the CSO and moves to Delano where he founds the Farm Workers Association.
  • 1962  The NFWA and Agricultural Workers Organizing Committee start the grape boycott.
  • 1965  In March, Chavez marches with 75 othersfrom Delano to the capital, Sacremento, 340 miles.
  • 1966  In February, Chavez begins his historic 25 day fast.
  • 1968  UFW declares National Grape Boycott Day.
  • 1970  In December, Chavez is imprisoned for challenging injuction against the boycott.
  • 1973  UFW celebrates first convention in Fresno.
  • 1975  California Supreme Court declares the short-handled an Unsafe Hand Tool thus banned by California law.
  • 1977  An agreement was reached that gave the UFW the sole right to organize farm workers.
  • 1984  Chavez announces a new grape boycott, this time focused on pesticides.
  • 1988  Chavez fasts for 36 days to protest pesticide use.
  • 1993  April 23, after a fast of several days, Chavez dies in his sleep of unknown cause.
  • 1994  Chavez posthumously receives the US Medal of Freedom from President Clinton.
  • 1996  Made a memorial of his history.
